Font Styling

Font weight refers to the thickness or boldness of the characters in a font. It is typically represented on a scale, with values ranging from light (thin) to heavy (bold). The weight can affect the readability and overall aesthetic of the text.

  • fw - is an abbreivation for font weight

Usage

  • Use fw-600 - The top-level heading, representing the primary title of the page or section.
  • Use fw-500 - A secondary heading, used for subsections under an h1 heading

Font Italics

Font italics refers to a type of text style where the characters are slanted to the right. It is commonly used in typography to emphasize certain words, phrases, or sections of text. Italics can convey meaning, express a different tone, or highlight specific content.

  • font-italics - is an abbreivation for font italics
