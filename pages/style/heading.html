Heading

Headings are text elements that mark the start of a section or subsections of content..

They typically represent titles or subheadings, guiding users through the content in a logical and hierarchical manner.

Headings help break up content into manageable sections, making it easier for users to skim or scan the page.

  • Use headings header-1 to header-5 to structure web pages, making content easier to navigate.
  • Assistive technologies use headings to allow users to navigate content efficiently. Examples-
      header-1: Page title
      header-2: Section headers
      header-3 to header-5: Subsection details
  • Use headings to draw attention to CTAs.
  • Organize content for easier comprehension.

Display

Display refers to a category of fonts that are designed specifically for use in larger sizes and high-visibility contexts, such as headlines, titles, posters, and advertisements. These fonts are intended to grab attention and make a visual impact, often prioritizing style, personality, and aesthetic appeal over long-term readability or legibility.

Display fonts are best suited for large textâ€”usually in sizes 18 points or larger. They are not designed for body text, where legibility and ease of reading are more important.

  • Use display to organize and clarify content text flow.
  • They are often used for headlines, titles, and other text that needs to be highlighted
  • Use the display property only when necessary; it is not mandatory to apply it in every situation.
