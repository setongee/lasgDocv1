Wrapper

Wrapper refers to an HTML element (often a div tag) that is used to "wrap" or group together multiple elements, typically for layout or styling purposes. Wrappers are commonly used to structure content on a page, manage layout, apply styling, or control the positioning of elements inside them.

Wrapper is a container (usually a div) that groups elements together for layout and styling purposes.
  • .wrapper-60 - The content takes 60% of the entire page.
  • .wrapper-70 - The content takes 70% of the entire page.
  • .wrapper-80 - The content takes 80% of the entire page.
  • .wrapper-100 - The content takes 100% of the entire page.

Use Cases of Wrapper

  • Use wrappers where necessary to avoid unnecessary nesting, which can make the markup harder to maintain.
  • Make wrappers flexible and adaptable by using responsive design techniques such as percentages, flexbox, or grid layouts.
  • A wrapper can be used to create consistent spacing around a page, maintaining uniformity throughout the layout.
